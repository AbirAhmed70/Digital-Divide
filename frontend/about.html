Digital Equality for all

About DigiWork

A community initiative to bridge the digital skills gap for young adults offering practical, free, Bangla-language workshops.It aims to empower young adults with the digital skills they need to succeed in the modern workforce.

Our Story

The Problem Is Visible Every Day in Jessore

Walk through any neighbourhood in Jessore and you'll meet educated, motivated young adults who are unemployed — not because they aren't capable, but because the jobs they're applying for expect digital skills they were never taught.

They don't know how to write a professional email. They've never built a CV in Word. They don't know how to search for jobs on LinkedIn or Bdjobs. And because they don't know what they don't know, they give up.

DigiWork exists to change that — one practical, free, Bangla-language workshop at a time.

Proximity to the Problem

Why This Works: We Are the Community

As social entrepreneur Bryan Stevenson said: "It's in proximity to injustices that we hear things we won't otherwise hear." The founder of DigiWork is a young adult from Jessore who understands this challenge personally. Our target group — unemployed and underemployed youth aged 18–30 — are our friends, classmates, and neighbours. This proximity ensures that every workshop is designed around their real needs, not assumptions made from a distance.
